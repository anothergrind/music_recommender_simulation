# Reflection: Profile Pair Comparisons

Comparing how each pair of profiles changed the top results and why the change makes sense.

1. High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: High-Energy Pop returns Sunrise City and Gym Hero because they are energetic and less acoustic, while Chill Lofi shifts to Library Rain and Midnight Coding because that profile rewards chill mood and higher acousticness.

2. High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock: Both profiles share high-energy songs, so Gym Hero appears in both lists, but Deep Intense Rock pushes Storm Runner to the top because it matches both rock genre and intense mood.

3. High-Energy Pop vs Conflicting Acoustic EDM: High-Energy Pop gets strong pop and happy matches, while Conflicting Acoustic EDM mostly gets energy-only matches because the dataset has no exact electronic plus energetic mood match.

4. High-Energy Pop vs Boundary Extremist: Boundary Extremist asks for medium energy and no mood target, so it introduces songs like Dusty Roads and Velvet Letters, while High-Energy Pop keeps very energetic tracks at the top.

5. Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock: Chill Lofi favors calm, acoustic songs with lower energy, while Deep Intense Rock favors high-energy, low-acoustic tracks, so their lists diverge almost completely.

6. Chill Lofi vs Conflicting Acoustic EDM: Both can reward acousticness, but Chill Lofi still wins with lofi/chill songs because it has direct mood and genre matches, while Conflicting Acoustic EDM gets weaker results from energy closeness only.

7. Chill Lofi vs Boundary Extremist: Chill Lofi focuses on chill mood plus acoustic texture, while Boundary Extremist ignores mood and lands on medium-energy songs across mixed genres.

8. Deep Intense Rock vs Conflicting Acoustic EDM: Both target high energy, so songs like Storm Runner and Neon Pulse appear in both outputs, but Deep Intense Rock scores higher confidence because it has direct mood and genre alignment.

9. Deep Intense Rock vs Boundary Extremist: Deep Intense Rock centers on extreme energy and intense mood, while Boundary Extremist drifts toward center-energy songs; this changes the list from heavy tracks to more balanced tracks.

10. Conflicting Acoustic EDM vs Boundary Extremist: Conflicting Acoustic EDM pulls very energetic songs despite acoustic conflict, while Boundary Extremist pulls mid-energy songs because its target energy is 0.5 and mood is neutral.
